# Adrien's Farm — Full Story Text

---

## Spread 1: The Overgrown Gate

Behind Grandmother's house, past the back steps and the old fig tree, there was a gate that nobody opened anymore.

Adrien opened it.

---

## Spread 2: What Grandmother Left

Inside: wild and tangled and alive. Tomato cages bent into sculptures. Sunflowers that had fallen and grown back sideways. Mint that had taken over everything.

*"She left it on purpose,"* Adrien thought. *"She was saving it for me."*

---

## Spread 3: The First Seed

In a jar on the windowsill, Adrien found seeds. Each one was labeled in Grandmother's handwriting: calendula, amaranth, black-eyed Susan, Cherokee purple.

Adrien chose one and pressed it into the soil with one finger. Just deep enough.

---

## Spread 4: Listening to the Soil

The earthworms came first. Then the beetles. Then a toad who lived under the rosemary.

*The soil wasn't empty. It was full.*

---

## Spread 5: The Long Sleep

Winter came, and the garden went quiet. Adrien worried.

But Grandmother's voice came back like it always did: *"Quiet isn't gone. Quiet is saving up.”*

---

## Spread 6: Something Stirs

In February, a green thread pushed through the cold soil. Just one. Then another.

Adrien ran inside to get a blanket. Just a small one. Just in case.

---

## Spread 7: Everything Returns

The garden remembered everything. Every seed Adrien had planted. Every weed they had pulled. Every bucket of water in the heat.

It had been keeping score. In the good way.

---

## Spread 8: The Harvest Belongs to Everyone

Adrien filled three baskets. One for the family next door. One for the food pantry on Carpenter Street. One to save seeds from, for next year.

The gate stayed open after that.
